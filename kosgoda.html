KOSGODA TURTLE HATCHERY

About the hatchery

Kosgoda Hatchery is a renowned sea turtle conservation project located in the coastal town of Kosgoda, Sri Lanka. Kosgoda is the one of the very few hatcheries where all the five species in the country make their nests. They are the Olive Ridleys, Leatherbacks, Green Turtles, Loggerheads and Hawksbills. The hatchery was established in the late 1980s as a response to the declining sea turtle populations and the increasing threats they faced along the coastal areas of Kosgoda. The hatchery began as a small-scale grassroots project driven by dedicated individuals and volunteers. Their major goal was to rescue sea turtle eggs from dangerous nesting places and transport them to a secure hatching area. To increase its influence and contribute to larger conservation programs, the hatchery has collaborated with government agencies, non-profit groups, and researchers. The Kosgoda Hatchery has also played an important role in promoting awareness about the need of sea turtle conservation among local communities, schools, and visitors. Kosgoda Hatchery is now a symbol of effective community-driven conservation initiatives.

About

The Sri Lanka Turtle Hatchery is a major sanctuary that contributes significantly to the preservation and maintenance of the diversified sea turtle population in the nation. The hatchery tirelessly protects sea turtle nests against predation and theft thanks to its enthusiastic and committed employees and volunteers, guaranteeing the survival of these amazing animals. The hatchery cares for the eggs and monitors hatching, ensuring that the young are released safely into the ocean and helping to protect these magnificent sea turtles and the sensitive marine habitats they call home.
