BENTOTA TURTLE HATCHERY

About the hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project is a renowned tourist attraction in Bentota. The gorgeous golden sands that entice you to our island also serve as an alluring nesting site for numerous endangered turtle species, ranging from the Olive Ridley to the Leatherback turtle. The hatchery serves as a sanctuary for wounded or sick turtles, offering them a place to heal. Skilled veterinarians and dedicated caregivers work diligently to rehabilitate these turtles, bringing them back to health before reintroducing them into their natural environment. The hatchery also acts as a nesting site for female turtles, sheltering their nests and ensuring the safe incubation of eggs until the hatchlings emerge. Visitors may participate in educational tours and interactive workshops to learn about the life cycle of turtles, their ecological value, and the continuous challenges they face on a daily basis. You start on a journey of exploration, compassion, and hope when you visit the Bentota Turtle Hatchery.

About

The Sri Lanka Turtle Hatchery is a major sanctuary that contributes significantly to the preservation and maintenance of the diversified sea turtle population in the nation. The hatchery tirelessly protects sea turtle nests against predation and theft thanks to its enthusiastic and committed employees and volunteers, guaranteeing the survival of these amazing animals. The hatchery cares for the eggs and monitors hatching, ensuring that the young are released safely into the ocean and helping to protect these magnificent sea turtles and the sensitive marine habitats they call home.
