Sri Lanka has a diversified sea turtle population, with five of the world's seven sea turtle species present in its seas. These turtles are a vital element of Sri Lanka's marine environment, helping to keep the ecosystem healthy and balanced. They help to manage the population of their victims, such as jellyfish and sponges, by servine as an indicator of the health of the marine environment.



WHY SEA TURTLES?



Sea turtles play a crucial role in the health of our marine ecosystem. However, it is disturbing to learn that 6 of the world's 7 species of sea turtles are in risk of extinction. This stresses on how essential it is to save these beautiful creatures and their environments.Here are just a few examples of how crucial sea turtles are to preserving our marine ecosystems:

  • turtles are significant scavengers who consume dead and rotting marine life. By doing so, the ecosystem is cleaned up and hazardous bacterial growth is prevented.
  • In order to stimulate the growth of vegetation and other species, sea turtles transfer nutrients from the ocean to the beaches where they lay their eggs.
  • By sea turtles consuming sea sponges, they help maintain the health of the coral reefs by minimizing the threat of the coral reefs being crowded out.

WHAT WE DO


We have a strong commitment to preserving sea turtles' health and their natural habitats. Through conservation programs, research, and public outreach, we strive to accomplish this aim.We work to advance sustainable practices and protect turtle nesting beaches


In addition, we are committed to raising public awareness of the threats to sea turtles and the need to conserve them. We are devoted to the preservation and defense of sea turtles and their natural environments here at our turtle hatchery. Our goal is to offer a secure habitat for sea turtle eggs to develop and mature in before reintroducing them to the ocean once they are healthy enough to thrive there. Additionally, we want to spread knowledge and increase awareness of the dangers that sea turtles face, such as habitat loss, pollution, and poaching. We think that by joining together, we can make a positive impact on sea turtle survival and the condition of our seas. In order to help us continue our efforts to safeguard these incredible animals, we thus invite you to support our hatchery and donate to our cause.

HOW YOU CAN HELP



Protecting sea turtles should not just the responsibility of governments or conservation organizations, but also of local communities. This is because sea turtles nest and feed in coastal areas that are often inhabited by people.

It is crucial for communities to take responsibility for safeguarding sea turtles by educating themselves on the threats faced by these species, and by taking steps to reduce their impact on turtle nesting and feeding grounds.Here are some ways in which the community can offer help work towards preserving these beautiful animals:

  1. By participating in beach cleanups,you can help create a safer environment for sea turtles and other wildlife.
  2. Your donations can help fund the operation of the hatchery as well as support research programs.
  3. By participating in volunteer programs that are offered by many of our sea turtle hatcheries you can directly contribute to the protection of sea turtles.
  4. By reducing plastic pollution and supporting eco-friendly packaging can help combat one of the biggest threats to sea turtles

About

The Sri Lanka Turtle Hatchery is a major sanctuary that contributes significantly to the preservation and maintenance of the diversified sea turtle population in the nation. The hatchery tirelessly protects sea turtle nests against predation and theft thanks to its enthusiastic and committed employees and volunteers, guaranteeing the survival of these amazing animals. The hatchery cares for the eggs and monitors hatching, ensuring that the young are released safely into the ocean and helping to protect these magnificent sea turtles and the sensitive marine habitats they call home.
