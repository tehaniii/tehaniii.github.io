Hikkaduwa Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka is a crucial conservation facility devoted to preserving sea turtles, who are in risk of extinction. This hatchery, which is situated in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa, is crucial for protecting the fragile population of these amazing creatures.In order to safeguard sea turtle nests and move them to a secure location where the eggs can hatch and the hatchlings can be released back into the ocean, the hatchery focuses on nest protection. Presentations and exhibitions that promote education help people understand the value of sustainability and conservation.


Things to do in the hatchery

Volunteer

Through its volunteer program, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka provides a wonderful chance for individuals to actively contribute to the protection of sea turtles. Participants may become immersed in the hatchery's daily operations by helping with chores such as turtle care, tank cleaning, and beach patrols to protect the safety of nesting turtles. Engaging with the hatchery's committed workers and observing the development of hatchlings instills even more dedication to the conservation of these vulnerable species. Volunteering at the Hikkaduwa Hatchery is a life-changing experience that allows you to help save sea turtles for future generations.

Release turtles

Visitors have the unique opportunity to pay to release hatchlings into the ocean directly at the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka. The opportunity to purchase and release turtles at the Hikkaduwa Hatchery offers a remarkable and significant experience that leaves visitors with a lasting perception of the importance of conservation and a commitment to the preservation of sea turtles for future generations.

Learn about sea turtles

Visitors may have a fascinating experience learning about several sea turtle species and the hatching process at the Hikkaduwa Turtle hatching in Sri Lanka. Each species, its distinctive traits, and the conservation difficulties they face are covered in detail by expert guides. The complex hatchery process, from the gathering and safeguarding of sea turtle eggs to the raising of hatchlings before their release into the ocean, is explained in detail to visitors. Visitors can gain information and become advocates for the conservation of these amazing species.

About

The Sri Lanka Turtle Hatchery is a major sanctuary that contributes significantly to the preservation and maintenance of the diversified sea turtle population in the nation. The hatchery tirelessly protects sea turtle nests against predation and theft thanks to its enthusiastic and committed employees and volunteers, guaranteeing the survival of these amazing animals. The hatchery cares for the eggs and monitors hatching, ensuring that the young are released safely into the ocean and helping to protect these magnificent sea turtles and the sensitive marine habitats they call home.
